Organization's Brief History:

PrintSari Corner was started in 2023 by my family because of the good business location in front of the municipal hall. I saw that many people come to this area every day for government transactions, and I thought this would be a good place to start a business. I noticed that people always needed to photocopy their documents like birth certificates and IDs when they apply for senior citizen cards or other government requirements. Since our shop is the nearest printing service to the municipal hall, customers naturally come to us for their xerox and printing needs.

The location also has a gymnasium nearby where basketball leagues, festivals, and concerts happen regularly. During these events, many people need snacks and drinks, and we are the closest store they can buy from. This made me realize we could serve both printing services and sell food items to the community.

When the business started growing, two of my siblings wanted to help by investing money in the business. They also help with operations whenever needed. My mother also wanted to be part of the business, so she became a co-owner as well.

Now, my mother and I check on the business every week to make sure everything is running well. We look at the sales, check if we have enough supplies, and see if our customers are happy with our services.

The business works well because of our location. We serve people who need government document services and people who want to buy snacks and drinks during community events. This makes PrintSari Corner an important part of the community.

Business Process:

Customer Purchase Process (Sari-Sari Store):

The customer selects snacks, drinks, or other items from the store shelves. The cashier manually checks the item price and calculates the total amount using a calculator or mental math. The customer pays cash, and the cashier gives change if needed. The cashier writes down what was sold on a bond paper list. No receipt is given to the customer.

Objects: Customer, Items, Cashier, Bond Paper, Calculator, Cash

Actions: selects, checks, calculates, pays, writes down

Printing Service Process:

The customer requests printing services (xerox, colored printing, lamination). The printing clerk asks for specifications like paper size, number of copies, and type of service. The printing clerk manually calculates the total cost based on memorized prices. The customer pays, and the printing job is completed. The cashier writes the service details and payment on bond paper.

Objects: Customer, Documents, Cashier, Printer, Lamination Machine, Bond Paper

Actions: requests, asks, calculates, pays, completes, writes

End-of-Day Process:

At closing time, the owner counts all cash in the container. The owner reads through the handwritten bond paper to see what items were sold during the day. The owner tries to estimate remaining stock by physically counting items on shelves. No proper record of daily sales, expenses, or profit is maintained.

Objects: Owner, Cash Container, Bond Paper, Items, Shelves

Actions: counts, reads, estimates, physical counting

5

Identified Problems:

  1. No Sales Tracking System: Everything is written manually on bond paper, making it difficult to track daily, weekly, or monthly sales accurately.
  2. Inventory Management Issues: The owner has no clear record of how many items are left in stock, leading to unexpected stockouts or overstocking.
  3. Manual Calculation Errors: All pricing and change calculations are done manually, increasing the risk of human errors and potential revenue loss.
  4. Financial Record Keeping Problems: At the end of each day, consolidating handwritten notes with actual cash is time-consuming and often inaccurate, making it hard to determine actual profit or loss.
  5. Mixed Business Tracking: It's difficult to determine which part of the business (sari-sari store vs printing services) is more profitable since all sales are recorded together on the same bond paper.

2.2 Use Case List

Sales Transaction System

A unified transaction system that processes both sari-sari store items and printing services in a single integrated interface. The system handles combined purchases where customers can buy store products and request printing services simultaneously. Each transaction is linked to a customer record and tracks detailed item information including purchase prices, selling prices, and profit margins. The system automatically updates inventory through Stock_Out records upon transaction completion.

Use Cases:

  • Process Customer Transaction: Handle combined purchases of store items and/or printing services, creating transaction records with customer linkage
  • Add Product to Transaction: Add product items to transaction with quantity, purchase price, selling price, subtotal, and profit calculation
  • Add Service to Transaction: Add printing service types to transaction via PrintOrder with cost breakdown (supply cost, ink cost, electricity cost, labor cost) and selling price
  • Calculate Transaction Totals: Automatically compute total amount from all transaction items, split store revenue and printing revenue, and calculate gross profit
  • Process Payment: Record cash received and payment method, calculate change for customer
  • Generate Customer Receipt: Create and print optional receipt with complete transaction details including itemized list
  • Track Transaction Items: Record individual items in Transaction_Item table with detailed pricing and profit information
  • Auto-Deduct Inventory: Automatically create Stock_Out records and reduce inventory quantities when transaction is completed
15

Dual Inventory Management System

A comprehensive real-time inventory tracking system that monitors stock levels for both sari-sari store products and printing supplies through Inventory_Item records. The system tracks stock movements via Stock_In and Stock_Out records, linking purchases to expense records for accurate cost tracking. Inventory items maintain current quantities, reorder levels, and expiry dates, with automatic deductions triggered by sales transactions. The owner manages stock additions and adjustments, while cashiers have view-only access to monitor current levels.

Use Cases:

  • Record Product Stock In: Create Stock_In records when receiving new product inventory with quantity, purchase price, expiry date, and link to expense
  • Record Supply Stock In: Create Stock_In records when receiving printing supplies with quantity and purchase price
  • Create Inventory Items: Generate or update Inventory_Item records linking to Stock_In with current quantities, reorder level, and expiry tracking
  • View Current Inventory: Display real-time stock levels for all products and supplies from Inventory_Item table (Owner and Cashier)
  • Adjust Product Quantity: Manually create Stock_In or Stock_Out records for inventory adjustments (Owner only)
  • Adjust Supply Quantity: Manually adjust printing supply quantities through Stock_In/Out records (Owner only)
  • Monitor Product Expiration: Track expiry dates in Inventory_Item and generate alerts for perishable products
  • Auto-Deduct on Sale: Automatically create Stock_Out records linked to transactions when items are sold
  • Link Purchases to Expenses: Connect Stock_In records to Expense records for financial tracking
16

Financial Report

An automated financial tracking and reporting system that computes business performance from transaction and expense data. The system provides Daily, Weekly, and Monthly views with revenue split between store and printing segments, expenses, transaction profit, and net profit after expenses. Reports include transaction counts, averages, and revenue breakdowns. Accessible only to the owner for business analysis and decision-making. Reports can be exported as PDF files.

Use Cases:

  • View Daily Performance: Display daily sales summaries with revenue and expense breakdown by date
  • View Weekly Performance: Show weekly business performance comparing store and printing revenues
  • View Monthly Performance: Display monthly business performance with trend analysis
  • Export Reports: Generate PDF files from Daily, Weekly, or Monthly reports for external use
  • Record Business Expenses: Enter and track business costs with category, source, description, amount, vendor, and date
  • Link Expenses to Purchases: Connect expense records to Stock_In records and vendors for inventory cost tracking
  • Calculate Net Profit: Compute net profit by subtracting total expenses from transaction profit
  • Track Expense Categories: Categorize expenses (utilities, supplies, maintenance, salaries, etc.) for detailed analysis

Product and Service Management System

A comprehensive management system for maintaining product catalog and service offerings. The Product module handles store items with categories, types, purchase prices, selling prices, and profit margins. The Service module manages printing services through a three-tier structure: Service (base service), Service_Type (specific variants with paper size and color mode linked to Service_Supply and Machine), and Service_Type_Cost (detailed cost breakdown). Soft-delete archiving preserves historical data while keeping active lists clean. Changes reflect immediately in the POS system.

Use Cases:

  • Manage Store Products: Add, update, or archive products with category, type, purchase price, selling price, and profit calculation
  • View Product Catalog: Display all active products with detailed information and current inventory status
  • Manage Services: Create and configure base printing services (xerox, printing, lamination)
  • Manage Service Types: Define service variants linking Service, Service_Supply, Machine with specifications (paper size, color mode)
  • Manage Service Type Costs: Configure detailed cost components (supply, ink, electricity, labor) and set selling prices
  • Configure Service Supplies: Add and manage printing supplies (paper types, toner, lamination film) with purchase prices and units
  • Assign Machines to Services: Link machines to service types and manage active/inactive/archived status
  • Archive Products/Services/Machines/Supplies: Soft-delete records while preserving historical data; restorable from Archives page
17

User Management System

A role-based access control system that manages user authentication, permissions, and activity tracking. The system supports two primary user roles: Owner (full access to all features) and Cashier (restricted access to POS and view-only inventory). User records include personal information, contact details, address fields, profile pictures, and account status (isActive). The Login_History table tracks all user sessions with login and logout timestamps. Users are linked to their actions throughout the system via userID foreign keys.

Use Cases:

  • Create User Account: Add new user with username, password, personal info, role assignment, and address details
  • Assign User Role: Set role-based access levels (Owner vs. Cashier) determining system permissions
  • Update User Information: Modify user credentials, personal details, contact info, address, and profile picture
  • Activate/Deactivate User: Set user isActive status to enable or disable account access
  • View User Accounts: Display all system users with roles, status, creation info, and contact details
  • Authenticate User: Validate login credentials via Supabase Auth and create Login_History record with login timestamp
  • Track User Sessions: Record all login and logout events with timestamps in Login_History table
  • End User Session: Process logout and update Login_History with logout timestamp
  • Track User Actions: Link user activities (transactions, stock movements, expense records) to userID

Customer Management System NEW

A customer record management system that maintains a registry of customers linked to sales transactions. Customers can be added manually at the POS or through the customer management interface. The system supports full contact information storage and links customers to their transaction history. Soft-delete archiving preserves customer records and their transaction associations while removing them from the active list.

Use Cases:

  • Manage Customer Records: Add, update, or archive customer records with name, email, phone, address, and notes
  • View Customer Records: Display all active customers with registration dates and contact information
  • Link Customer to Transaction: Associate a customer record with a sales transaction at POS via customerID
  • Archive Customer: Soft-delete customer records while preserving transaction history linkage

Vendor Management System NEW

A vendor directory system that maintains records of suppliers and service providers. Vendors are linked to expense records, enabling accurate tracking of which supplier an expense was paid to. This allows the owner to analyze spending per vendor and manage supplier relationships. Soft-delete archiving removes vendors from the active list while preserving their linkage in historical expense records.

Use Cases:

  • Manage Vendors: Add, update, or archive vendor records with name, contact number, email, and address
  • View Vendor Records: Display all active vendors with contact details
  • Link Vendor to Expense: Connect expense records to specific vendors for cost tracking and supplier analysis
  • Archive Vendor: Soft-delete vendor records while preserving links in historical expense records

Archive Management System NEW

A centralized archive management interface that provides the owner with a view of all soft-deleted records across five entity types: Products, Machines, Service Supplies, Vendors, and Customers. Instead of permanent deletion, records are marked as archived (is_archived = true), preserving data integrity and historical references. The owner can restore any archived record back to active status at any time.

Use Cases:

  • View Archived Records: Browse all archived items across five tabs (Products, Machines, Service Supplies, Vendors, Customers)
  • Restore Archived Record: Reactivate any archived record (set is_archived = false) returning it to the active list

Activity Log System NEW

An automated audit trail system that logs all significant user actions across the application. Every operation — from creating transactions and updating inventory to recording expenses and managing users — generates a corresponding Activity_Log entry with the action type, description, performer identity, timestamp, and optional metadata. The owner can view and filter the complete audit history by action category (Transactions, Inventory, Products, Users, Expenses, Login) and by date range.

Use Cases:

  • Monitor User Activity: Automatically log all system actions with actor, timestamp, action category, and metadata
  • View Audit Trail: Display complete activity history with action type and date filtering
  • Track Action Categories: Organize activity by category: transactions, inventory, products, users, expenses, login
  • Filter Activity Log: Filter log entries by action type (Today, Last 7 Days, Last 30 Days) and search by performer or description

Use Case Description

3.1 Sales Transaction

Use Case NameProcess Sales Transaction
ScenarioCashier records the customer's order and processes the payment
Triggering EventCashier inputs orders in the POS
Brief DescriptionCashier lists ordered products/services in the POS. The system computes the total, splits store and printing revenue, and records the transaction.
ActorsCashier, Customer
Related Use CaseProduct Inventory Management, Service Supply Inventory Management, Customer Management
PreconditionsProducts and services must exist in the system; inventory must have sufficient stock
PostconditionsTransaction details are saved; inventory is updated; stock_out records created
Flow of ActivitiesActor:
1. Cashier selects customer (optional) and lists orders in the POS.
2. Cashier confirms order, enters payment method, and cash received.

System:
1.1 System checks stock availability for each item.
1.2 System computes subtotal, store revenue, printing revenue, and gross profit.
1.3 System records the payment and calculates change.
1.4 System saves transaction and creates stock_out records to deduct inventory.
Exception ConditionsIf the stock is unavailable, the system notifies the cashier to remove or replace the item.

3.12 Activity Log NEW

Use Case NameView Audit Trail
ScenarioOwner reviews all system actions taken by users
Triggering EventOwner opens the Activity Log page
Brief DescriptionThe system automatically logs all significant user actions. The owner can view, filter, and search the complete activity history to monitor operations and detect issues.
ActorsOwner, System
Related Use CaseUser Management, Sales Transaction, Expense Management, Inventory Management
PreconditionsActivity_Log table exists; at least one action has been performed
PostconditionsFiltered activity log displayed to owner
Flow of ActivitiesActor:
1. Owner opens Activity Log page.
2. Owner applies filters (action category: Transactions, Inventory, Products, Users, Expenses, Login) and/or date range (Today, Last 7 Days, Last 30 Days).

System:
1.1 Queries activity_log table with filters applied.
1.2 Displays matching records with action name, description, performer, and timestamp.

System (Background):
2.1 On every significant action, system inserts Activity_Log record with actionID, description, performedBy, performedByID, timestamp, and optional metadata.
Exception ConditionsIf no records match the filter criteria, the system displays an empty state message.
